Thank you for viewing this humble portfolio! Believe it or not, it has been 20 years in the making. I would like to get started by outlining some UX fundamentals I have learned during my journey into UX Design. You will find summarizations below.

Empathizing

According to Dictionary.com, empathy is defined as "the psychological identification with or vicarious experiencing of the feelings, thoughts, or attitudes of another." When we empathize, we truly understand where someone is coming from as if we have been in those same situations. Empathizing with our users puts us in the users' shoes, viewing a reality through their eyes, and in their mind. Empathy leads to discovery.

Defining

During the defining phase, designers put together the research they have created and gathered during the empathize phase. They analyze their observations, synthesize the research to define the core problems, and then identify a problem and user to focus on.

Ideating

During the ideation phase, designers are ready to start generating ideas. This can include brainstorming, sketching, and using a lot of sticky notes.

Prototyping

During the protyping phase, designers will produce a number of low-cost, low-fidelity prototypes based on ideas generated during the previous phases. Designers may create user flows, sketch paper prototypes, and begin building high-fidelity prototypes.

Testing

During the testing phase, the entire testing model is based around an iterative process. The designers will create a minimum viable product (MVP) which they then test. Based on the test the designers will iterate the MVP and redesign it. They will then test it again and so on.
