Project Summary

Portland foodies want a more unique experience when discovering local food carts around Portland. The food coma app highlights a comprehensive list of food carts available for dining around the Portland area.

My Responsibilities

This project had three team members. We were all responsible for the creation of the deliverables. The wireframes and prototypes were created by me.

Objective 1
Understand what motivates people to explore different food carts around Portland

Objective 2
Understand what a “unique dining experience” means to people eating at food carts in Portland

Objective 3
Understand what steps people take in order to discover new and different food carts around Portland

Research Objective

To discover what the user values about the Portland foodie scene, to discover what steps the user takes when looking to explore new and interesting food carts, and to discover their feelings gaining a deeper connection to the chefs behind the food carts.

Kathy
1. How often do you eat out?

a. No that much anymore, probably about twice a week.



2. How do you typically find food carts in Portland?

a. I usually stumble upon it by driving by a food cart pod, or word of mouth, or a recommendation and I’ll try it out that way. Sometimes I’ll go online to look but usually, I see them when I’m out and about in Portland.



3. What do you think about the Portland food scene?

a. It’s amazing! I love how that even during COVID, there are still new places popping up. It’s not only unique but resilient to survive during such a tough time.



4. How do you learn about local chefs and their restaurants in Portland?

a. Sometimes I see something on EaterPDX or on Instagram, but I don’t normally read a ton on them. I don’t go out of my way to find info unless I happen to read an article. I always find them super interesting though.



5. What is most important to you when dining out or getting takeout?

a. Probably how fast the food can be ready, the quality of the food. The dining experience used to be a lot more important to me, but since COVID hit I don’t expect some extravagant experience, I just want to have a safe and enjoyable one.



6. How often do you explore new food carts around Portland?

a. Maybe once or twice a month.



7. Describe what a unique dining experience means to you.

a. It depends on what I’m feeling. Like if there’s an occasion, I expect a really cool one. Having unique flatware is nice, everything is plastic now since COVID so we don’t get to use cool ceramic bowls and such.



8. Tell me about your most recent dining experience at a food cart.

a. It was awesome, the people were super nice and the food was delicious. It came super fast even though it seemed like they had a ton of to-go orders. The outdoor space that they’re a part of was really spacious and comfortable.



9. What is your ideal dining experience?

a. Honestly, I miss family-style dining. That’s on the top of my list and is something I miss. That’s my ideal one.



10. Do you prefer to eat indoors or outdoors?

a. I don’t mind either!



11. Do you prefer dining at a restaurant or eating at a food cart and why?

a. I prefer dining at a restaurant, especially if I’m going with a group. It’s easier to get service. And sometimes when I do go to a food cart their hours have changed and I realize they’re closed even though it’s not posted online. So restaurants tend to be more established in that department sometimes.



12. What do you like about food carts?

a. I like how casual it is and the variety of foods to choose from. I like how everyone can get whatever they want and we can all still enjoy a meal together.



13. What do you enjoy about exploring the food cart scene in Portland?

a. I like exploring the pod itself and how the owner has made it welcoming and unique. Whether there’s a beer garden or cocktails that are available. I’m in the process of building a food cart pod with my partner so these are all things I’m taking note of.



14. What do you like about yelp?

a. I don’t use it all that much, but when I do, it can be helpful. But sometimes it can be filled with jargon and poor reviews that I’m not sure I can trust. It can feel like a lot to look at sometimes as well and the hours aren’t always updated properly on there which can be annoying. I do like how you can filter out by most recent.



Mackie
1. How often do you eat out?

a. About 3 times a week.



2. How do you typically find food carts in Portland?

a. Probably like yelp or just driving by a pod. Sometimes word of mouth



3. What do you think about the Portland food scene?

a. I think it’s great. I feel like there’s a big variety and the aesthetic of restaurants is nice. COVID makes things harder to enjoy it all though.



4. How do you learn about local chefs and their restaurants in Portland?

a. Instagram, Facebook, word of mouth. Sometimes an article that’s posted.



5. What is most important to you when dining out or getting takeout?

a. That the food tastes good. The atmosphere is important to me and the service.



6. How often do you explore new food carts around Portland?

a. Not super often honestly, we mostly go to restaurants. I’d like to explore more though.



7. Describe what a unique dining experience means to you.

a. Probably something unusual in terms of environment or atmosphere, or the food.



8. Tell me about your most recent dining experience at a food cart.

a. I went to a sushi food cart and it was fine. It was only for lunch, no one was there. It was fast and I enjoyed it overall. It was okay.



9. What is your ideal dining experience?

a. Either rooftop location or somewhere with a lot of sun and greenery. Or a really aesthetically pleasing restaurant with bold colors and great cocktails. That’s all very important to me. Good service.



10. Do you prefer to eat indoors or outdoors?

a. Indoors unless it’s summer and I’m getting beer or cocktails out on the patio.



11. Do you prefer dining at a restaurant or eating at a food cart and why?

a. Dining at restaurants because I feel like there’s usually more space to eat with friends, which can be hard to find at food carts sometimes. Sometimes there’s not enough space.



12. What do you like about food carts?

a. I like that they’re usually quick, with minimal interaction with people. The variety of food, the options. It’s usually pretty affordable.



13. What do you enjoy about exploring the food cart scene in Portland?

a. Finding new places to eat, new styles of food, or trying something I’ve never had before. I like that there’s a lot of Asian-inspired food carts.



14. What do you like about yelp?

a. I like that they have a map that you can search for places on.



15. What do you dislike about yelp?

a. They’re not accurate with “open now” and whether places offer outdoor dining. The hours aren’t always accurate.



Ryan
1. How often do you eat out?

a. I eat out probably 3 times a week.



2. How do you typically find food carts in Portland?

a. Through food influencers, like Bill Oakley who is really into fast food and food carts. Instagram or EatPDX lists that come out. I don’t use yelp so much for food trucks but I use it for other things.



3. What do you think about the Portland food scene?

a. I think it’s really good. You can basically eat tasty bar food anywhere or elevated dining experiences. Something that’s lacking is healthy plates, I feel like it’s either something super fried or something super leafy and bland.



4. How do you learn about local chefs and their restaurants in Portland?

a. I feel like I don’t learn a ton about the chefs unless they’re the ones running their Instagrams or I read an article about them.



5. What is most important to you when dining out or getting takeout?

a. It depends. I go for different experiences. But right now with COVID, I’d just like to be able to sit somewhere with solid service and is socially distanced for the most part. Obviously, the food needs to be good.



6. How often do you explore new food carts around Portland?

a. Not that often honestly, I feel like I used to think the food cart thing was cool when I first moved here. I explored it more often when I used to work downtown, but not so much now that I’m WFH. I don’t really go out of my way to find a food cart unless something is recommended to me. I probably go to one like every 6 months now. I try new restaurants way more often than a new food cart.



7. Describe what a unique dining experience means to you.

a. It’s everything from a level of thoughtfulness. The first place I think of is Farm House Thai. I love the classic Thai tablecloths and all the lovely colors around the restaurants. The cocktails are served in cool glasses and it feels like you’re in Thailand, it’s very much more of an experience than just getting food. Feels like you’re doing something different than just order basic noodles.



8. Tell me about your most recent dining experience at a food cart.

a. I went to Never Coffee and got breakfast next door at the food cart park. It was okay nothing special.



9. What is your ideal dining experience?

a. It involves trying lots of different types of food, tasty drinks, great music, laid-back servers, having the owner or bartender giving you a stand-out experience. When we were at Lazy Susan the owner came up to my partner and me and brought us a port wine on the house just for us to try and that was extremely special and such a small kind gesture. That made us feel at home and something we talk about a lot, it was truly special.



10. Do you prefer to eat indoors or outdoors?

a. Well right now, outdoors. But pre-COVID, depends on weather and time of day.



11. Do you prefer dining at a restaurant or eating at a food cart and why?

a. I prefer restaurants because I like sitting down and planning to stay there for a while. I like being able to hang out and not feeling like I have to walk around.



12. What do you like about food carts?

a. I like them because a lot of times they serve interesting food or have interesting takes on food. Good grounds to explore new types of food. The people you’re with can all get something they want and you can all still enjoy a meal together.



13. What do you enjoy about exploring the food cart scene in Portland?

a. I enjoy the unexpected nature of it. There’s a lot of randomness to it, and it’s cheap for the most part so you can always find something you’re looking for.



14. What do you like about yelp?

a. One thing I hate about it is that one bad review can ruin my judgment on finding a place to eat. You’re at the mercy of a bad review. But what I like about it is that it does help me find places to eat and make a decision. It can be hard to trust the positive reviews sometimes because it feels like they’re incentivized to write them.

Portland area residents feeling adventurous trying out new food trucks need more information before trying them because they love supporting the local food scene, but they want a good idea about where to go.

Users need help finding unique food carts with updated hours and locations and more accurate reviews because they do not find yelp useful.

During user interviews, we discovered that the majority of individuals who ate at food carts found out about them by driving by or word of mouth and primarily learned about local chefs from online articles or social media.

Therefore, we believe that users need a better way of finding unique dining experiences at food carts and that we might be able to help if we developed an app that provided a more in-depth look at Portland food cart chefs, as well as updated hours and locations of food carts and accurate reviews on the dining experience and food quality.
